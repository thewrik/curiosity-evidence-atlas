# RentControl Reference Notes

Reference:
[Rent Control: The Ceiling Trap](https://mason.gmu.edu/~atabarro/RentControl/)

## What To Borrow

### One Argument, Many Angles

The page does not feel like a dashboard. It feels like an essay. Every chart,
photo, quote, and interactive widget is subordinate to one argument.

For this repo, every essay should start with one plain-language claim:

> The thing people believe is partly true, but the missing mechanism changes
> the conclusion.

### Chapter Rhythm

The reference uses chapters to create a guided reading path:

1. the intuitive appeal
2. the mechanism
3. a vivid case
4. repeated empirical evidence
5. what works instead
6. sources

For our essays, use:

1. the curiosity
2. the common story
3. the mechanism
4. the data test
5. the counterexample
6. what changed my mind
7. source trail

### Sidenotes And Source Texture

The page uses sidenotes to keep the prose flowing while still letting the
curious reader audit details. This is better than a dashboard footnote dump.

### Interactive Mechanism First

The supply-demand widget appears before the empirical tour. That is important:
the reader gets the mechanism before the cases. In our essays, build a small
"mechanism toy" early, then use FactIQ charts to test whether the toy survives
contact with data.

## What To Avoid

- Do not copy the exact visual design or rent-control content.
- Do not make every page beige, sepia, and serif by default.
- Do not turn the essay into a collection of charts with paragraphs between
  them. The story is the product.
- Do not hide methodology until the end. Let important caveats surface as
  sidenotes when they matter.
