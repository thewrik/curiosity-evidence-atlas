# Playbook: Patient Capital

Trigger questions:

- "Do patient investors have an observable edge?"
- "Are markets too short-term?"
- "How does a long horizon change valuation?"
- "Can we see the cost of short-termism in data?"

## Required Coverage

A complete answer should separate four concepts:

1. Time horizon: how long the investor can wait.
2. Business durability: whether cash flows can survive the wait.
3. Market pressure: what forces other holders to sell or reprice.
4. Opportunity cost: rates, inflation, and liquidity conditions.

## FactIQ Data To Look For

- market data and fundamentals for selected companies
- earnings-call mentions of short-term and long-term language
- interest rates and inflation from official macro sources
- sector-level employment, sales, or productivity where relevant
- benchmark drawdowns and recoveries

## Chart Ideas

- A timeline of drawdown, valuation multiple, and later fundamentals.
- A bar chart comparing earnings-call language: "near-term" versus "long-term."
- A rates overlay showing when patience became more expensive.
- A table of claims from investor letters mapped to later observable outcomes.

## Guardrails

- Do not equate later price increase with thesis correctness.
- Separate company fundamentals from stock returns.
- Watch survivorship bias: the remembered patient theses are often the winners.
- If using investor letters, label them as primary-source claims, not truth.
