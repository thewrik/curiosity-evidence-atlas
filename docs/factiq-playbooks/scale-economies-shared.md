# Playbook: Scale Economies Shared

Trigger questions:

- "When does scale benefit customers?"
- "Do companies share scale economies?"
- "Why can a great business keep prices low?"

## Required Coverage

1. Unit economics: cost, price, margin, volume.
2. Competitive structure: why savings are shared or retained.
3. Customer outcome: lower prices, better quality, faster delivery, or more
   selection.
4. Durability: whether the behavior persists across cycles.

## FactIQ Data To Look For

- company fundamentals and margins
- retail sales and category CPI where relevant
- productivity, wages, and employment by sector
- earnings-call language around price, value, scale, efficiency, and customers

## Chart Ideas

- Margin versus price index over time.
- Sales growth versus category inflation.
- Earnings-call language frequency around "value" and "price."
- A mechanism toy showing when scale is shared versus retained.

## Guardrails

- Customer value is not only price.
- Company-level margins can rise even while some categories get cheaper.
- A low price can reflect subsidy, cross-subsidy, or predation; do not assume
  benevolence.
