# Playbook: Housing Scarcity

Trigger questions:

- "Why are rents high?"
- "Does rent control work?"
- "Is housing expensive because of demand or supply?"
- "What happens when a city restricts building?"

## Required Coverage

A complete answer should cover:

1. Renter burden and price level.
2. Housing supply: permits, starts, completions, and vacancy.
3. Demand pressure: population, household formation, income, and jobs.
4. Policy mechanism: price ceiling, zoning, tax, subsidy, or voucher.
5. Distribution: who benefits, who is excluded, and who faces higher prices.

## FactIQ Data To Look For

- Census housing starts, permits, completions, vacancies
- BLS CPI rent and owners' equivalent rent
- BEA income and population where available
- metro or state data if available in the catalog
- World Bank or IMF for cross-country comparisons

## Chart Ideas

- Housing units permitted versus population or job growth.
- CPI rent versus wage growth.
- Vacancy rates before and after policy changes.
- Controlled versus uncontrolled market outcomes, if a reliable source exists.

## Guardrails

- Distinguish first-generation rent freezes from modern stabilization.
- Do not infer causal effects from a simple before/after chart.
- Separate affordability for incumbents from affordability for movers.
- Make legal and institutional caveats visible in sidenotes.
