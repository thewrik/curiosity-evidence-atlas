# Product Brief

## Name

Curiosity Evidence Atlas.

Working description: a personal visual essay studio for turning recurring
questions into beautiful, audited, data-backed arguments.

## Audience

The first audience is you: someone who wants to use curiosity as a serious
research engine. The second audience is a thoughtful reader who may not share
the priors, but is willing to follow a well-built trail.

## Core Promise

Pick a question that keeps coming back. The repo helps turn it into:

- a public essay
- a claim ledger
- a data appendix
- interactive charts
- source notes
- a reproducible research trail

## Design Principles

- Start with curiosity, not content strategy.
- Make one argument at a time.
- Separate claims from vibes.
- Let charts make claims, not merely decorate topics.
- Keep provenance visible.
- Treat caveats as intellectual hospitality.
- Prefer small, sharp interactive models over heavy dashboards.

## The Three-Layer Model

### 1. Narrative Layer

The public essay: chapters, prose, photos, diagrams, interactives, and the
reader's emotional path.

### 2. Evidence Layer

The claim ledger: each factual statement has status, source, confidence,
dataset, and chart/report linkage.

### 3. Reproduction Layer

The FactIQ and local workflow: SQL, series IDs, calculations, ChartSpecs,
published report links, and notes about what could not be verified.

## Initial Topic Families

- Patient capital: does a long horizon show up as an observable edge?
- Scale economies shared: when do companies give efficiency back to customers?
- Regulation and industry structure: when does a rule reshape competition?
- Housing scarcity: where do price ceilings, supply limits, and mobility collide?
- Inflation experience: why does lived inflation differ from official inflation?
