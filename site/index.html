A worked example, not a template

What can the Nomad letters dataset answer?

Using the extracted data from 24 Nomad Investment Partnership letters, this page asks what the archive already knows: which ideas repeat, which companies carry them, where the richest research leads are, and what needs external market data next.

Question Set I

The Dataset Already Answers These

The useful long-term idea is not "make a site." It is to turn a private reading trail into a queryable map of claims, examples, and research leads.

Question Set II

What Ideas Were Most Persistent?

The extracted themes make the philosophy measurable.

The strongest signal is not a single investment. It is recurrence. Consumer value proposition appears in every letter in the dataset; management alignment and patience are nearly as persistent. This gives you a concrete starting point for a long-term research project: trace whether those ideas predicted later outcomes.

Question Set III

Which Companies Carry The Philosophy?

The companies are not just tickers. They are reusable case studies.

Costco, Amazon, Stagecoach, Berkshire Hathaway, Matichon, Marathon, and Wal-Mart recur across multiple years. That means the next research pass should not start from a blank market screen; it should start from first-mention dates, extracted thesis passages, and later outcomes.

Question Set IV

Where Should The Second Pass Start?

The archive can rank its own research leads.

A useful reading system should tell you where to go next. Here the best second-pass candidates are the letters with the densest mix of claims, extracted artifacts, and company examples.

Long-Term Use

What This Becomes With FactIQ

The local dataset tells us what Nomad believed. External data can test what happened next.

I drilled into the FactIQ repo and built one concrete next research question: the Scale-Sharing Test.
